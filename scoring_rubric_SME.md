# Video Interview Scoring Rubric — SME (Subject Matter Expert / CTE)

## Instructions
Score each criterion **1 to 4**. A score of 1 or 4 is expected whenever the evidence clearly supports it — do not default to 2 or 3. Record the total and the per-criterion scores in the candidate's notes.

---

## Scoring Rubric

### 1. Role Fit and Relevant Experience (gating criterion)

*What it measures:* Does their actual background meet the core bar for the role? Program-level CTE work plus compliance knowledge — beyond classroom teaching.

| Score | Description | Behavioral Signals |
|:---:|:---|:---|
| **4** | Directly on point; could contribute almost immediately. | Has held a program coordinator, director, or pathway lead role for a CTE program — beyond classroom teaching. Has personally navigated at least one of: Perkins funding compliance, articulation agreement negotiation, or industry certification approval. Could review a CTE course against its compliance requirements with zero ramp. |
| **3** | Clear, relevant experience that maps to the role. | Has worked at the CTE program level (e.g., department lead, pathway committee, curriculum coordinator) alongside or beyond classroom teaching — OR is a classroom teacher who has personally developed CTE course curriculum from scratch AND navigated at least one funding or compliance process (e.g., securing materials funding, identifying certification requirements, coordinating with CTE supervisors on compliance gaps). Understands that CTE courses carry funding and compliance obligations and has direct experience meeting them. |
| **2** | Adjacent experience only; would need significant ramp. | Is primarily a CTE classroom teacher who delivers existing curriculum: has deep pathway content knowledge but has not developed CTE course materials from scratch and has not navigated funding, articulation, or certification compliance — not even informally. Understands the academic standards their courses target but views their role as delivery, not design or compliance. |
| **1** | No relevant experience to the core bar. | No CTE experience at any level. Background is from non-CTE K-12 education, higher education without CTE relevance, or an unrelated field. Cannot describe the structure of a CTE pathway or its compliance requirements. |

---

### 2. Domain Judgment

*What it measures:* Understands the CTE compliance landscape — standards, funding mechanisms, articulation agreements, industry certifications.

| Score | Description | Behavioral Signals |
|:---:|:---|:---|
| **4** | Deep, nuanced understanding; gives concrete examples. | Demonstrates layered knowledge of the CTE compliance landscape: names and distinguishes at least two of (state CTE standards, Perkins Act funding requirements, industry certification body criteria, articulation partner requirements). Gives a specific example of a compliance gap they identified — what the consequence was and how it was resolved. Understands that different compliance layers may conflict and can explain how to prioritize. |
| **3** | Solid, specific understanding. | Understands that CTE courses must meet requirements beyond academic content standards. Names at least two types of requirements a course must satisfy (e.g., state standards alignment AND certification body criteria, or articulation partner terms). Has reviewed or developed materials against at least one compliance type and can describe what they looked for. |
| **2** | General awareness, few specifics. | Knows CTE has "standards" and "requirements" but uses these terms vaguely without naming specific frameworks, funding categories, or certification bodies. Cannot give a concrete example of evaluating a course against a specific compliance requirement. Answers at the level of general curriculum quality rather than CTE-specific compliance. |
| **1** | Little understanding shown. | No awareness of the CTE compliance landscape. Reviews a CTE course as if it were a general academic course — mentions content accuracy or grammar with no reference to standards, funding, articulation, or certification. Cannot name a single CTE-specific requirement type. |

---

### 3. Process and Methodology

*What it measures:* Structured, systematic approach to the task they describe.

| Score | Description | Behavioral Signals |
|:---:|:---|:---|
| **4** | Rigorous and thoughtful about edge cases. | Describes a systematic course review process with explicit steps across multiple compliance layers (e.g., standards check → funding/certification requirements → articulation terms → gap documentation). Addresses edge cases: what if requirements conflict? How do you prioritize gaps for the writing team? Process is designed to scale across many courses. |
| **3** | Clear, repeatable approach. | Describes a clear review sequence with at least two named steps across different requirement types. Can explain how they would ensure gaps become actionable revision notes, not just a list of problems. *Example that earns a 3: "I check standards alignment first, then verify the certification requirements, then document each gap with a specific revision instruction for the writing team" — a multi-step compliance review, regardless of how casually phrased.* |
| **2** | Some structure, but gaps remain. | Would review a course but without a structured sequence across requirement types. Might describe checking standards generally without a step-by-step approach or a method for turning gaps into actionable direction. |
| **1** | No clear method. | Would "read through the course" or "look for problems" without any structured framework. Cannot describe a sequence of checks or how they would communicate gaps to a writing team. |

---

### 4. Communication

*What it measures:* Clear, concise, organized, easy to follow. Note: a narrative or story-format Q1 answer (describing a past experience) is not inherently "roundabout" — setting context before the key signal emerges is normal. The question is whether the listener can follow the through-line without getting lost. Repetitive phrasing that restates the same point without advancing it, or an answer that circles back without landing on a concrete example, IS "roundabout" regardless of format. Spoken filler words ("you know," "so yeah," "things like that") are artifacts of spoken delivery — they do not move a score down. Score 3 if the substance is followable and the listener can track each answer's through-line, even when the delivery is casual.

| Score | Description | Behavioral Signals |
|:---:|:---|:---|
| **4** | Crisp, confident, and persuasive. | Uses specific, precise language. Structures each answer so a listener can follow without effort. Demonstrates the communication quality needed to write clear, actionable revision direction for a curriculum writing team — can turn a compliance gap into a specific, prioritized instruction rather than a vague critique. |
| **3** | Clear and well organized. | Answers each question directly. Easy to follow without re-listening. No significant tangents. |
| **2** | Understandable with some effort. | Answers the question but takes a roundabout path. Some sentences are unclear or run on. Requires inference to fill gaps. |
| **1** | Hard to follow or rambling. | Loses the thread mid-answer, contradicts themselves, or cannot complete a thought. Would struggle to write a clear revision note a writing team could act on. |

---

### 5. Instruction-Following and Professionalism

*What it measures:* Answered all three questions, on time, professional delivery. Note: this criterion measures question coverage and example quality — not structural organisation. If two answers blend together in delivery but both questions are substantively addressed with concrete examples, score based on coverage. Organisational clarity belongs under Communication, not here.

| Score | Description | Behavioral Signals |
|:---:|:---|:---|
| **4** | Exceeded expectations; polished and prepared. | Answered all three questions with depth and specificity. Came prepared with a concrete example for each question. Closing is professional and on-point. |
| **3** | Met all expectations. | Answered all three questions completely. Professional tone. No meaningful gaps in content. |
| **2** | Met most expectations. | Addressed all questions but one answer is thin, off-topic, or vague. |
| **1** | Missed questions or late. | Skipped or clearly missed one or more of the three questions. Submission is informal or incoherent. |

---

## Scoring Rules

**Maximum Score:** 20 points

* **Advance to interview:** Total of 14 or higher, AND Criterion 1 (Role Fit) scored 3 or higher.
* **Hold or borderline:** Total of 11 to 13. Discuss before deciding.
* **Decline:** Total of 10 or lower.
* **Automatic decline (hard fail):** Regardless of total score, automatically decline if the candidate:
    * Did not answer all three questions.
    * Has no experience meeting the role's core bar.
    * Cannot communicate clearly enough to follow.
